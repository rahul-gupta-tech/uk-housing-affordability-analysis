# uk-housing-affordability-analysis
Combined UK property transaction and earnings data to measure housing affordability across the UK. Developed SQL analyses and Power BI dashboards to identify regions where median house prices are most affordable relative to local incomes.

Key headlines
-------------

National Affordability Challenge

The median house price across England in 2025 was £287,000, compared with a median annual salary of £38,800, resulting in a national affordability ratio of 7.8x annual earnings.

Affordability Varies Significantly by Location

Housing affordability differs substantially between districts, with London and parts of the South East exhibiting the highest house price-to-salary ratios.

London Dominates the Least Affordable Rankings

The least affordable districts in England are concentrated in and around London, with Kensington and Chelsea showing the highest affordability ratio at approximately 20x annual earnings.

Regional Patterns are Clearly Visible

The geographical analysis reveals a strong north-south divide, with many southern districts experiencing significantly higher affordability pressures than much of the Midlands and Northern England.
